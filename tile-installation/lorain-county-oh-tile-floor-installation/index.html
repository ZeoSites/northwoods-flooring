What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is typically denser and less porous than tiles made of ceramic. This makes porcelain tile more durable and less susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also not as prone to host microbes and mildew.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a premium clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a harder, more dense tile that is less absorbent and more immune to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, kitchens, and baths.

Ceramic tile is crafted from a natural clay material that is fired at a lesser temperature than tiles made of porcelain. These conditions results in a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Ceramic tile is a suitable choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
