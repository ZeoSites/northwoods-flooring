What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and less porous than ceramic tile. These properties makes porcelain tile more durable and less susceptible to stains and water damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also not as prone to host microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than ceramic tile. This yields a tougher, denser tile that is less absorbent and more resistant to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal selection for high-traffic areas such as entryways, cooking areas, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is crafted from a organic clay material that is baked at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. This yields a softer, more porous tile that is prone to stains and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good selection for low-traffic areas such as bedrooms and living rooms.
