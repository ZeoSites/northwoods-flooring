What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is typically denser and less porous than tiles made of ceramic. This make it porcelain tile longer-lasting and more resistant to stains and water damage. Thanks to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to harbor bacteria and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a refined clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a tougher, more dense tile that is less porous and more immune to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal selection for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a softer, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to stains and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
