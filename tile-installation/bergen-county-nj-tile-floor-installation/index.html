What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and less porous than ceramic tile. These properties makes it more durable and more resistant to staining and water damage. Thanks to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host bacteria and mildew.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a premium clay material that is baked at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. This results in a tougher, denser tile that is less porous and more resistant to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal choice for high-traffic areas such as entryways, kitchens, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. This results in a more yielding, more porous tile that is more susceptible to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as bedrooms and family rooms.
