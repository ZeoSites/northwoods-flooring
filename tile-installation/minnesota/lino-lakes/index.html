  1. 1. Adjust the Tile Membrane's Size as Required
  2. 2. Spread and Comb the Mortar for the Tile Membrane
  3. 3. Place the Tile Membrane in Position
  4. 4. Waterproof the Membrane Seams
  5. 5. Create a Starting Point for Laying Tile
  6. 6. Inspect the Arrangement for Tile Placement
  7. 7. Prepare the Mortar for the Floor Tile
  8. 8. Start Placing the Tiles
  9. 9. Clean and Level the Tile as You Go
  10. 10. Make Tile Cuts as Needed
  11. 11. Let the Mortar Set

After finishing the earlier tasks, the professional will then apply the grout, let it set, and finalize the installation.

What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and less porous than tiles made of ceramic. These properties make it it more durable and less susceptible to staining and water damage. Due to its limited porosity, tile made of porcelain is also less likely to harbor bacteria and mildew.

Porcelain tile is produced from a refined clay material that is fired at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. This yields a harder, denser tile that is less porous and more resistant to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an ideal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is fired at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. This yields a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
