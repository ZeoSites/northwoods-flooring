What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and less porous than ceramic tile. These properties make it porcelain tile more durable and more resistant to staining and moisture damage. Thanks to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also not as prone to harbor bacteria and mildew.

Porcelain tile is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions yields a tougher, more dense tile that is less absorbent and more immune to wear and tear. Porcelain tile is an ideal selection for high-traffic areas such as entryways, kitchens, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a organic clay material that is baked at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions yields a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and moisture damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable selection for low-traffic areas such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
