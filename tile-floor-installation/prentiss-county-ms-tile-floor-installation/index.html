What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. These properties make it it more durable and less susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Due to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also not as prone to harbor microbes and mildew.

Porcelain tile is crafted from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions yields a harder, more dense tile that is less porous and more immune to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an ideal selection for high-traffic areas such as entryways, kitchens, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is crafted from a natural clay material that is baked at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions yields a more yielding, more porous tile that is more susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable selection for low-traffic areas such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
