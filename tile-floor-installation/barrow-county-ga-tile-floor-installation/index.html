What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is typically denser and less porous than ceramic tile. This makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and less susceptible to staining and water damage. Due to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also not as prone to harbor bacteria and mildew.

Porcelain tile is crafted from a premium clay material that is baked at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. This yields a tougher, more dense tile that is less absorbent and more resistant to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an optimal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Ceramic tile is crafted from a organic clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. This yields a softer, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and water damage. Ceramic tile is a good choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
