What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is typically denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. This makes it more durable and more resistant to staining and moisture damage. Due to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to harbor microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a refined clay material that is fired at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a tougher, denser tile that is less absorbent and more immune to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal selection for high-traffic areas such as entryways, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is crafted from a organic clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a more yielding, more porous tile that is prone to staining and moisture damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as bedrooms and family rooms.
