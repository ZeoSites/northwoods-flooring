What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is typically denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. This make it it more durable and more resistant to staining and moisture damage. Due to its limited porosity, tile made of porcelain is also less likely to host bacteria and mold.

Porcelain tile is crafted from a premium clay material that is baked at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. This results in a tougher, more dense tile that is less porous and more resistant to wear and tear. Porcelain tile is an ideal selection for high-traffic areas such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Ceramic tile is crafted from a organic clay material that is baked at a lower temperature than tiles made of porcelain. This results in a softer, more porous tile that is prone to staining and moisture damage. Ceramic tile is a good selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
