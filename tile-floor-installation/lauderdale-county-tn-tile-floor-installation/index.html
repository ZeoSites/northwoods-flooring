What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and less porous than ceramic tile. This makes porcelain tile more durable and less susceptible to staining and water damage. Due to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a refined clay material that is fired at a higher temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions yields a harder, more dense tile that is less porous and more resistant to wear and tear. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Ceramic tile is crafted from a organic clay material that is fired at a lesser temperature than tiles made of porcelain. These conditions yields a softer, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to staining and water damage. Ceramic tile is a suitable choice for low-traffic areas such as bedrooms and family rooms.
