What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. These properties makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and less susceptible to staining and water damage. Due to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mold.

Porcelain tile is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions yields a tougher, denser tile that is less absorbent and more immune to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an optimal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a organic clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions yields a softer, more porous tile that is more susceptible to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good choice for low-traffic areas such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
