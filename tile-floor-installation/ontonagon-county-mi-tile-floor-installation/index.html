What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. These properties makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and more resistant to staining and water damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mildew.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a premium clay material that is fired at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions yields a harder, more dense tile that is less absorbent and more resistant to wear and tear. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is crafted from a organic clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions yields a softer, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good choice for low-traffic areas such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
