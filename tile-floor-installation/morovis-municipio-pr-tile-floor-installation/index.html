What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. These properties makes it more durable and less susceptible to staining and water damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mold.

Porcelain tile is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a elevated temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a harder, denser tile that is less absorbent and more resistant to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an ideal selection for high-traffic areas such as entrances, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a softer, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable selection for low-traffic areas such as bedrooms and family rooms.
