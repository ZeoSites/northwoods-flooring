What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. These properties makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and more resistant to staining and water damage. Thanks to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host bacteria and mildew.

Porcelain tile is produced from a premium clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions results in a tougher, more dense tile that is less porous and more resistant to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an optimal choice for high-traffic areas such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is baked at a lower temperature than tiles made of porcelain. These conditions results in a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
