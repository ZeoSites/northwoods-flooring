What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is typically denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. These properties make it it longer-lasting and more resistant to staining and water damage. Due to its low porosity, tile made of porcelain is also less likely to host microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a elevated temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions yields a tougher, denser tile that is less porous and more immune to wear and tear. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Ceramic tile is produced from a organic clay material that is baked at a lower temperature than tiles made of porcelain. These conditions yields a softer, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and water damage. Ceramic tile is a suitable choice for low-traffic areas such as bedrooms and living rooms.
