What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. This make it it longer-lasting and less susceptible to staining and moisture damage. Due to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a harder, denser tile that is less porous and more resistant to wear and tear. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to staining and moisture damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.

What floor tiles make a room look bigger?
