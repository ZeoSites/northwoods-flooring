What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is typically denser and less porous than tiles made of ceramic. This makes it longer-lasting and less susceptible to staining and water damage. Due to its limited porosity, tile made of porcelain is also not as prone to host bacteria and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a refined clay material that is fired at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a tougher, more dense tile that is less porous and more resistant to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, cooking areas, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a natural clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than tiles made of porcelain. These conditions results in a more yielding, more porous tile that is prone to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
