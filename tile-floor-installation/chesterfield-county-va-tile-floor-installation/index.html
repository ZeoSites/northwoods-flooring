What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is typically denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. This makes it longer-lasting and more resistant to staining and moisture damage. Due to its limited porosity, tile made of porcelain is also not as prone to harbor microbes and mildew.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a premium clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions yields a harder, more dense tile that is less absorbent and more immune to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal selection for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, kitchens, and baths.

Ceramic tile is produced from a organic clay material that is fired at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions yields a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is prone to staining and moisture damage. Ceramic tile is a suitable selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as bedrooms and family rooms.
