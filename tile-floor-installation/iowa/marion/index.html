Which procedure should be followed when installing floor tiles?

Skilled workers in this domain, like our personnel, have several standards and processes which they adhere to when executing top-notch work for our clientele, these comprise:

  1. 1. Cut the Tile Membrane to Size
  2. 2. Spread and Comb the Mortar for the Tile Membrane
  3. 3. Place the Tile Membrane in Position
  4. 4. Waterproof the Membrane Seams
  5. 5. Create a Starting Point for Laying Tile
  6. 6. Inspect the Arrangement for Tile Placement
  7. 7. Prepare the Mortar for the Floor Tile
  8. 8. Begin Laying Tile
  9. 9. Clean and Level the Tile as You Go
  10. 10. Cut the Tile as Needed
  11. 11. Let the Mortar Set

After completing the previous steps, the professional will then put in the grout, allow the grout to cure, and complete the installation.

What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and more impervious than tiles made of ceramic. This makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and more resistant to stains and water damage. Thanks to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also not as prone to harbor microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. This results in a tougher, denser tile that is less absorbent and more resistant to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal selection for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, kitchens, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is crafted from a natural clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than tiles made of porcelain. This results in a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is prone to stains and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
