What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is typically denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. These properties makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and less susceptible to stains and water damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to harbor microbes and mildew.

Porcelain tile is crafted from a premium clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. This yields a tougher, denser tile that is less porous and more immune to wear and tear. Porcelain tile is an optimal choice for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, cooking areas, and baths.

Ceramic tile is crafted from a organic clay material that is fired at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. This yields a more yielding, more porous tile that is prone to stains and water damage. Ceramic tile is a good choice for low-traffic areas such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
