  1. 1. Adjust the Tile Membrane's Size as Required
  2. 2. Evenly Spread and Comb the Mortar for the Tile Membrane

What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is commonly denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. This make it porcelain tile longer-lasting and more resistant to staining and moisture damage. Due to its low porosity, tile made of porcelain is also less likely to harbor bacteria and mildew.

Porcelain tile is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions results in a harder, more dense tile that is less absorbent and more resistant to wear and tear. Porcelain tile is an ideal choice for high-traffic areas such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Ceramic tile is produced from a natural clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than tiles made of porcelain. These conditions results in a softer, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to staining and moisture damage. Ceramic tile is a suitable choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
