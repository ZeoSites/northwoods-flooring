What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is typically denser and less porous than tiles made of ceramic. This makes porcelain tile more durable and less susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Due to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mold.

Porcelain tile is produced from a refined clay material that is baked at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. These conditions results in a harder, denser tile that is less porous and more resistant to wear and tear. Porcelain tile is an optimal selection for high-traffic areas such as entrances, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a organic clay material that is baked at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a softer, more porous tile that is more susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as bedrooms and living rooms.
