What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is typically denser and less porous than ceramic tile. These properties makes porcelain tile longer-lasting and less susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, tile made of porcelain is also less likely to harbor bacteria and mildew.

Porcelain tile is crafted from a premium clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions results in a tougher, more dense tile that is less porous and more immune to damage from use. Porcelain tile is an optimal selection for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Ceramic tile is crafted from a organic clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a softer, more porous tile that is more susceptible to stains and moisture damage. Ceramic tile is a suitable selection for low-traffic areas such as bedrooms and living rooms.
