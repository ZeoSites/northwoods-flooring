What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Porcelain tile is typically denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. This make it porcelain tile more durable and more resistant to staining and water damage. Thanks to its low porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to host microbes and mildew.

Porcelain tile is produced from a premium clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. This yields a harder, more dense tile that is less porous and more immune to wear and tear. Porcelain tile is an ideal selection for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entryways, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a organic clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than tiles made of porcelain. This yields a more yielding, more absorbent tile that is more susceptible to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.
