What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and more impervious than ceramic tile. These properties make it porcelain tile longer-lasting and more resistant to staining and moisture damage. Thanks to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to harbor microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is produced from a premium clay material that is fired at a elevated temperature than ceramic tile. These conditions results in a harder, denser tile that is less porous and more resistant to wear and tear. Tile made of porcelain is an ideal selection for locations with frequent foot traffic such as entrances, cooking areas, and baths.

Tiles made of ceramic is produced from a organic clay material that is fired at a lower temperature than porcelain tile. These conditions results in a more yielding, more porous tile that is prone to staining and moisture damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a good selection for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and family rooms.

What floor tiles make a room look bigger?

A 16″ or 18″ tile will ‌give a room a larger feeling than a 12″ tile. One of the primary reasons for this is the minimization of grout lines.
