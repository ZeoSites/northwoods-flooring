What is the difference between porcelain tile and ceramic tile?

Tile made of porcelain is commonly denser and less porous than tiles made of ceramic. These properties makes porcelain tile more durable and more resistant to staining and water damage. Due to its limited porosity, porcelain tile is also less likely to harbor microbes and mold.

Tile made of porcelain is crafted from a refined clay material that is fired at a higher temperature than tiles made of ceramic. This yields a tougher, denser tile that is less porous and more resistant to damage from use. Tile made of porcelain is an optimal choice for high-traffic areas such as entryways, cooking areas, and bathrooms.

Tiles made of ceramic is crafted from a natural clay material that is fired at a lesser temperature than porcelain tile. This yields a softer, more porous tile that is more susceptible to staining and water damage. Tiles made of ceramic is a suitable choice for locations with infrequent foot traffic such as sleeping areas and living rooms.
